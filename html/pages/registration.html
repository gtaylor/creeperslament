Registration

Access to Creeper's Lament is granted by getting your username added to our server's white list. We restrict access in order to keep the griefers out, and to make sure our server can scale up as needed.

Hosting a server in a commercial data center costs a good bit of money. In the interest of fairness (this should not be one person's burden) and making sure this server remains, even as people come and go, we split the hosting costs equally between all players. This allows us to beef up our server as we add more people, or save money by scaling it back when people leave. In this manner, we can maintain a near-constant price point, and see the same excellent performance and reliability we do now.

Why play on this server, versus other [free] ones?

Running a Minecraft server [well] is a tricky endeavor, and is not something you want to run on your home connection. Minecraft is extremely sensitive to latency and irregularities in the host and client's internet connections. Running the server in a data center offers the greatest volume of bandwidth, at the most reliable level.

The server application itself is also very inefficient, requiring us to seek out optimizations for speed on the Linux and Java VM levels to help lessen or prevent the infamous stuttering we see on Minecraft multiplayer. The administrative staff handles this for you, so you can break blocks better/faster. Other things like automated backups and prompt Minecraft Server updates are things that you probably don't think about, but notice when they are not done and disaster strikes.

We all chip in to help provide a fast, secure, safe, and reliable Minecraft server. Since we are a private server, we are protected from griefers, and your creations will be free from harm.

Keeping the lights on

This Minecraft server is currently hosted on Linode, and is on the 768 MB plan. This costs about $30/month. Rather than one person shouldering the burden, we have elected to distribute the costs between the people who play on this server. There are some additional benefits to this setup, aside from the one person thing:

  • The more people that sign up, the cheaper it gets for everyone.
  • As the subscription base grows, we can upgrade the Linode VM to a higher plan, giving us more resources.

It is important to understand that we are not a for-profit business. We are all aiming to pay the least amount possible to keep the lights on. We are not selling work, we make no claims of ownership to the world.

How to start playing

If you are interested in playing on our server, please fill out a trial request form. We can arrange a week-long trial if you'd like to play a while before deciding whether to toss your money in the pot. You will not be able to connect to the server until we have white-listed your account, so make sure to get in touch so we can get your username added.

If you'd like to become a more permanent fixture on the server, click the Subscribe button below and set up a recurring payment. We will adjust the rate (and communicate before doing so) as people come and go.

You may unsubscribe at any time by clicking on the following link, or by cancelling within your PayPal account.

What subscribing does get you

  • Access to our private Minecraft server.
  • Protection from the all-too-common griefers on public servers.
  • Warm fuzzies for helping keep a private, protected, zippy Minecraft server running for the enjoyment of yourself and others.
  • If interest in the server ever drops to the point where we all decide to stop paying for it, you can get a copy of the world files to play locally, or on your own server.

What subscribing does not get you

  • Elevated privileges (admin rights). These are potentially dangerous, prone to accidental breakage, and improper use can anger other players.
  • Any kind of ownership of the server or world. The world files are licensed under Creative Commons, and the server is rented.
  • A pony.

The golden rule

Absolutely no destructive griefing: No burning down of houses, destruction of another player's stuff, and etc. If what you're doing causes someone else's playing time to be wasted, it is not OK.
